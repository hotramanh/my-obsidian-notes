# Big C's of Matching
1) Communication - being honest about what you can afford, handle, availability
	- Following up every month then quarterly. will be informed if being rematched
2) Consistency - not getting burned out, show up when saying so
	- Even if not knowing the right answer, 
3) Collaboration - make sure to keep in check with the agency
	- Can ask agency for supplies for craft night 

# Boundaries
- Setting expectations and following through
- Protect you and child (and family)
	- lets them know what they can/can't do 
- Examples
	- No eating in the car
	- Let them know what the plan is 
	- Ask them 
- Sometimes have to repair trust when boundaries are pushes 
- Also have to set boundaries with Families 
	- Don't act as a babysitter
	- Don't give them money 
- hold them accountable, but don't feel responsible for their issues (STAY CONSISTENT)

# What if's?
- little is quiet/zero conversation? 
	- can yap for them, shift to activities/parallel play 
	- Going for a walk, Driving in the car, doing crafts 
- little asks uncomfortable questions?
	- boundaries pretty important 
	- direct them to asking their parents (esp abt sexuality, religion, etc.)
- little wants gifts/to be bought stuff?
	- can pay for meals/activities, but don't make it super monetary 
- little wants to stay at your home?
	- keep out of house for 3 months 
- little wants to trade social media?
	- probably shouldn't follow until 3 months 
- little runs off? 
	- call them back
	- call 911 if you can't find them, call the parent as well 
- not much progress with the relationship with the little?
- disagree with the parents on how to address behavior?
	- ultimately parent has the final say, but can bring it up with the parent
	- can involve match support to do good cop/bad cop 
	- have a lot of grace 
- close the match? 
	- if you have to move, closure is super important. definitely let them know 
	- let them know what you enjoyed and stuff
	- it's okay to only be with a little for a season, they'll understand 
- volunteer more? 
	- more than 1 little
	- match events (e.g. ice-cream social)
	- ambassadors for the program 
- custody issue (ex-husband, stalker)?
	- call 911 if kiddo is in danger
	- follow/up with match support (who can follow up with family) if feeling uneasy
