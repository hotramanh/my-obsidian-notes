estate attorney specializations
- **middle-market planning** - avoiding probate, ensuring smooth transition, long-term care planning, knowledge of special needs
- **probate/administration** - death busywork, getting the executor/administrator appointed, transferring assets
- **elder law** - senior issues. medicaid planning, probate avoidance, benefits, guardianships
- **special needs** - blends with elder law, as special needs and senior needs overlap. 
- **tax/high net worth** - worth tens of millions. may have millions in death taxes
- **private client/family office** - for WEALTHY ppl. not just estate planning. preparing a lease, purchasing a jet, finding best DUI attorney
- **litigation** - when there's serious dispute (invalidating a will, enforcing a trust)

**IMPORTANT:** As long as they know their limits = meaning they know what they don't know and will tell clients when to seek out someone with more knowledge. 

- should you do online will/will templates: maybe, but probably not bc there's no real guidance
- should you get estate planning from a financial advisor: fine as long as they stay in their lane and are willing to work with other specialists 

However, when dealing with a solo attorney, and particularly a very old attorney, you might want to ask if the attorney has a plan in place for any sensitive papers that the attorney may hold on to.

- attorney needs to be licensed in your state. if they offer any advice and they're not licensed in ur state. THATS ILLEGAL
- awards are worthless and pay to play. certifications are fine if state sponsored 
- What to look for in an attorney: 
	- empathy, knowledge, and clarity. they sell peace of mind
	- they ask questions. why do you need a trust? counselors, not just a form filler

# pricing 
- if it’s a relatively simple revocable trust package (without add-ons and bells or whistles), the price should range from about $2500 to $7500 anywhere 
	- includes: healthcare directive, durable power of attorney, and power over Will.
	- optional: a certificate of trust, assignment of personal property, agent for disposition of remains, and a deed, 
- price mostly depends on attorney network and the volume of clients they get
