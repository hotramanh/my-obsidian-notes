[methodology of analysis](https://docs.google.com/document/u/0/d/1eH_Vr6FFaSo_HwnEclbkgvf9xf3JUpjKBgB0RcaDDmk/mobilebasic "https://docs.google.com/document/u/0/d/1eH_Vr6FFaSo_HwnEclbkgvf9xf3JUpjKBgB0RcaDDmk/mobilebasic)
- ask what not why 
	- define foundation to build from
	- don't just use surface level definitions when defining ideas
	- need to understand the concept on a deeper level (e.g. with lived experiences, how it connects to other ideas)
	- use ur own experiences before using other ppl's definitions 
		- aligns with your needs for that definition 
		- "true-er" to your experience
		- "think about it yourself, develop an idea that’s unique to YOU, to how YOU think," ... otherwise "you lose the opportunity to discover more of an understanding of your own unique worldview"
	- don't make definition about purpose (chair is not something you sit on, it's a "constructed raised surface of a size and constitution designed and built to support a sitting human being")
- continuously refine theories and models 
	- synthesizing (seemingly) opposing viewpoints can help 


first scenes
- find what makes your story unique, and distill that into its first scene. find themes and make audience feel them 
	- severance “by disconnecting you from your identity and emotions, you are susceptible to control”
	- slay the princess "people become what you expect"
- try SO hard to integrate it into the story and have it make sense
- make it at least confusing as possible. clear structure
	- define agency 
- examples:
	- indiana jones is abt ADVENTURE and finding TREASURE
	- arcane is about the two sisters being torn apart by war
	- fmab is about two brothers trying to bring their mom back to life (and alchemy)
	- severance is about the employees trying to escape lumon (sampler platter of whole story arc )
	- the matrix is about philosophy and also kickass fighting scenes 
- first line can be echoer, idea that will echo throughout the entire story
	- the prestige "are you watching closely?"
	- severance "who are you?"
	- dune "dreams are messages from the deep" (but also with eerie otherworldly sfx)
	- star wars "epic as fuck main theme"
	- slay the princess "this is a love story"

- gives form to real feelings we have abt lives/worlds
