# HSA
- triple tax advantaged (no taxes on money going in, while growing, or being taken out)
- only available for HDHP
- After age 65, the HSA can also be used for non-medical expenses, with such withdrawals taxed at your regular tax rate (not even tax advantaged anymore!!)

## 401k Traditional vs Roth IRA
- [bogleheads comparison article](https://www.bogleheads.org/wiki/Traditional_versus_Roth)
- [r/personalfinance comparison article](https://www.reddit.com/r/personalfinance/wiki/rothortraditional/) 
### factors when selecting
- roth = taxed contributions
- 401K = taxed withdrawals 
- usually ppl go all in traditional, sometimes go all in roth 
- sometimes do a 50/50 split or max out both (good for tax diversification)
	- **2024 max contribution limits**: 7k for roth, 23.5k for 401k 
- things to consider:
	- iras are individually controlled
	- 401ks have rules from employer/provider
	- marginal tax rate *now* vs estimated marginal tax rate at *withdrawal* (i.e. in retirement)
		- tax rate now > tax rate future => traditional
		- tax rate now < tax rate future => roth
		- things that raise/lower your marginal tax rate
			- state tax codes (florida ⬇️ vs california ⬆️) ([bogleheads article](https://www.bogleheads.org/wiki/State_income_taxes))
			- ⬆️ income level  (and filing status)
			- ⬇️ tax deductions (including from IRA contributions up to a certain income level)
				- deduct more with more kids/dependents 
		- [marginal tax rate calculator](https://www.ameriprise.com/financial-news-research/financial-calculators/marginal-tax-rate-calculator)
	- you can withdraw from roth contribution if shit hits the fan
	- you don't know what the tax rates of the future will be 
	- you can convert a traditional account => roth, but not the other way around 
	- most high earners are likely to be at a lower marginal tax rate in retirement

# Glide Path
> "the process by which a target date fund changes its asset allocation among risky assets (which can include stocks, international stocks, REITs and commodities) and lower risk assets such as bonds, inflation indexed bonds and money market funds over a time horizon." 
> [- Bogleheads](https://www.bogleheads.org/wiki/Glide_paths)

basically change from being more aggressive (stocks) to more conservative (bonds) the closer you get to your "deadline"
