> Viscose isn't evil, people just don't understand it and hence have a bad experience with it.
> 
> I've given long rants explaining the intricacies before but TL:DR
> 
> Rayon is the overarching term, they are typically labeled by process or brand name.
> 
> If it's simply named rayon/viscose generally avoid unless you know specifically it won't be an issue in the case you need it for, this is first gen fabric made using harsh chemicals and is very weak comparatively.
> 
> Cupra/cupramonium is almost non existent but for the most part the same applies.
> 
> Modal is so-so, this is the point where depending on use case I'd argue it is worth trying out, especially if you can't find something better at a decent price. It's stronger and less toxic but still not amazing.
> 
> Micromodal is supposed to be a brand name for a special type of modal but I've seen brands use seemingly non brand name micromodal. In either case it's an upgraded form of modal. I'd buy, it's what I use for clothing because the next forms are either nonexistent or prohibitively expensive for me in these form factors.
> 
> Lyocel is the newest version, the toughest and most sustainable, using non toxic and recycled materials to create. Definitely buy.
> 
> Tencel is one of the biggest brand names of lyocel, it's theoretically even more sustainable and better made but I wouldn't go out of my way to find it.
> 
> Most of my rayon materials are Lyocel/tencel, I tend to start my searches there for any new product and settle for micromodal/modal if I can't find those.
> 
> There are further intricacies, brand names, properties(some variants are softer, more stretch, hold water better, better for different applications, etc) but for the most part the form factor and specific way a company uses the material matters more.
> 
> People buy the cheapest, shittiest viscose from a Chinese sweatshop and complain that it's low quality. No fucking shit dumbass. It's not a plastic(see discussion below) it's non toxic to the end user(id still always wash new clothes with a natural detergent regardless of fabric type) and it biodegrades at similar rates to cotton. Really the _only_ bad thing about it is the old cheap versions are shitty and toxic to manufacture. Like every material in existence.
> 
> Lyocel is probably one of the most sustainable fabrics we have today. I also personally love its properties.
