# 1: preparation 
## why
- to become mindful about how we spend **time** and **energy** by focusing on what's meaningful
- bullet journal = OFFLINE to avoid information overload. be present and aware
- decide what's important to you and work on those goals 
- track decisions made + actions taken that led you to where you are
- cutting out the stuff you dont actually care about 

## decluttering mind 
- decisions require focus/time/energy (all limited resources!)
	- decision fatigue => decision avoidance
- need to reduce decisions to do things on purpose 
- how to reduce decisions? 
	- externalize thoughts 
	- make **mental inventory**
		- list currently working on, should be doing, want to do 
		- only keep things that matter (important to me/someone i love) or are vital (will have serious consequences if i don't do them)

## notebooks 
- phones are seriously distracting 
- we spend SOO much time in front of screens
- notebooks are offline and flexible (apps are either a pain in the ass to use e.g. excel or only good at one thing e.g. notes app)
- can also use notebook and apps in conjunction. notebook is a new toolkit 

### handwriting
- it takes longer to write things so it's better at helping you remember things 
- taking notes = have to reword to be shorter + more effective. actively glean main ideas and most important parts 
- easy to mistake convenience for efficiency 

# 2. the system
- combo of task list/journal/planner
- each concept is modular (but most effective when all together) so take what works and leave the rest. work towards implementing the whole incrementally 

## key concepts
- **index** - locate content
- **future log** - store tasks/events that fall outside current month
- **monthly log** - overview of time/tasks for current month. also monthly mental inventory
- **daily log** - catchall for rapid logging thoughts throughout the day
- **rapid logging** - short form notation 
	- `-` note
	- `○` event
	- `•` task
	- `X` task complete 
	- `>` task migrated
	- `<` task scheduled
	- ~~`•` task irrelevant~~
- **collections** - modular building blocks to store related content. core ones are index/future log/monthly log/daily log 
- **migration** - monthly process of filtering out meaningless content

## topics and pagination
- frame content to log by naming topic  (at top of page)
	- identify/describe content
	- serve as opportunity to clarify intention
	- set agenda for content 
	- decide if it's actually important to you
- "**Oct 13, Meeting 4 notes**" vs **"10.13.TH / Acme Co. / Website Relaunch / User Feedback"**
	- "10.13.TH (month/date/day) 
	- Acme Co. (client name)
	- Website Relaunch (project name)
	- User Feedback (meeting priority)"
- be sure to add to index 

## bullets
- balance brevity + clarity
- tasks / events / notes 

### Tasks 
- record of tasks
	- easier to remember when away from journal.
	- create archive of actions. super helpful during reflections 
- task types:
	- `•` task not yet completed 
	- `X` task complete 
	- `>` task moved forward into next monthly log or specific collection 
	- `<` task moved backward into future log 
	- ~~`•` tasks that don't matter anymore~~
#### subtasks / master tasks 
- indent dependencies e.g. 
- do the thing
	- dependency for doing the thing 
- if task has a ton of subtasks, might be time to move it into a collection. can also make task to move stuff into collection 
### Events
- denoted with `○`
- experiences that are scheduled or logged after occurring 
- should be as objective/brief as possible 
	- hard to verbalize complex feelings so just leave that out!
	- can nest note bullets underneath if you want to add details. still keep brief though 
- memories are unreliable (negativity bias, rose colored glasses, missed red flags, etc.)
- unpack events ASAP 
- scheduled events can be added to future log if outside month
### Notes
- denoted with `-`
- info to remember that isn't actionable
- let stuff sink in for a couple minutes after meeting/class/etc
	- review notes, see what questions pop up. curiosity is strong source of motivation 
- try to personalize notes to make them memorable e.g.

> “Some groups of animals enjoy unusual colloquial names. A group of flamingos is called a flamboyance of flamingos. A group of crows is known as a murder of crows.... A group of pugs is known as a grumble of pugs.”
- **Ineffective note:** some animals have names.
- **Effective Note:** A group of pugs is called a grumble! A murder of crows!

### signifiers / custom bullets
- can come up with whatever to make it yours. 
	- `*` - priority. use sparingly
	- `!` - inspiration
	- `ø` - task assigned to someone else. turn into `X` when complete
- especially good for recurring tasks/events
	- e.g. `♪` for music practice 
- but try to keep to minimum (pain in the ass to remember your 50 custom bullets)

## collections 
- modular setup
- organize and collect related information
- interchangeable, reusable, and customizable 
- stack = portfolio of collections 
- 4 core collections 
	- daily log 
	- monthly log
	- future log
	- index 
### daily log
- collect daily deluge to focus on tasks 
- document tasks/experiences
- safe space to express all mind/thoughts => daily log becomes record of state of being (good for reflection)
- gives context for daily life to be more deliberate with decisions 
- continuously log days, don't need to make new page for each day 

### monthly log 
- left side is calendar - acts as timeline. better to log events *after* they happen 
- right side is mental inventory. 
	- go through previous month and see what tasks still need doing. 
	- add important tasks to new monthly log tasks (more in migration)

## Migration
- transfer bujo content from one place to another by rewriting 
- weeds out distractions. have to consider if we WANT to rewrite each thing to readd to todo list 

#### monthly migrations 
- make new monthly log
- incomplete tasks from previous month
	- totally fine
	- evaluate each one
		- does it still matter? is it still vital? 
		- if yes to both, why is it incomplete? (curious and niceys, not judgey)
		- in either case, add it either to 
			- new monthly log
			- custom collection
			- future log 
- also evaluate tasks from future log 

#### yearly migrations
- get new bujo EVERY year 
- decide what's worth keeping from previous bujo

# 3. the practice

- do morning/nightly reflections 
	- morning to plan
	- night to review tasks + acknowledge wins 
	- we do this during monthly/yearly migrations 
- find meaning
	- ikigai . “Your ikigai is at the intersection of what you are good at and what you love doing,”
	- There is no intellectualizing what resonates with you, and that’s why it’s so hard to define. When it reveals itself, you feel it.
	- curiosity from exciting potential 
	- what would my path be if i did everything i was curious about? no concern with risks 
- goals
	- harness curiosity while reducing failure 
	- goals should be inspired by your felt experience
		-  “I want to make enough money to pay off my student loans, buy a two-bedroom house for my parents to retire in, and cover my kids’ education.”
	- turn big boy goals into smaller more achievable ones 
		- learn to cook => learn knife skills 
	- get into what/why of goals
		- What about this goal sparked my curiosity?
		- What motivated me to want to invest my time and energy here?
		- What am I trying to accomplish?
		- What will it require?
		- What is my definition of success with this goal?
